01 · The Logo

The mark is the climb.

The mark reads as value rising in stages to a single gold point: the exit. Four ascending bars are the work of the phases; the rising line and the gold summit node are the destination. Navy carries the bars, gold marks the exit, the same two colors that anchor The Walton Group. The wordmark is set in a clean grotesque for a modern, product feel, while headlines elsewhere use Georgia, TWG's heading face.

Clear space & minimum size

Keep clear space around the lockup equal to the height of the mark on every side. Minimum width: 132px on screen, 1.1in in print. Below that, use the mark only.

SmartExitPlan

What not to do

  • Do not box the mark in a gold or navy fill. The bars sit on transparent, white, or navy surfaces only.
  • Do not recolor the summit node away from gold. The node is the one fixed accent.
  • Do not stretch, rotate, or reorder the bars, or flip the climb to descend.
  • Do not set the wordmark in Georgia. Georgia is for headlines; the wordmark stays grotesque.
  • Do not place the lockup on a busy photo without a navy or paper field behind it.

04 · The Signature

The climb to exit.

One visual idea anchors the brand: enterprise value rising in stages to the exit. Reuse it as the hero, in pitch decks, and in reports. The bars build, the gold line rises, the node marks the exit. The numbers on it are the real per-phase value impacts of the system, never invented figures.

Built from the methodology, not decoration.

The four building bars map to Foundation, Operations, Growth, and Excellence. The labels are the system's documented value impacts. When the climb appears, it should always tell the truth about the phases it represents.

05 · Voice & Tone

Plain, precise, and never hyped.

The audience is a serious owner about to make the largest financial decision of their life. Earn trust the way a senior advisor does: say specific, defensible things, and let the numbers carry the weight. Never use an em dash; en dashes and hyphens only.

Principle 01

Specific beats clever.

Name the real thing. A documented value impact lands harder than a slogan, because the reader can check it.

Reduces the due diligence friction that discounts a valuation.
Unlock your business's true potential.
Principle 02

Confident, not loud.

State outcomes plainly. No exclamation marks, no urgency theater, no fear-based statistics we cannot defend.

Value is built in years, not the final quarter.
Don't wait! 80% of businesses never sell!!
Principle 03

The system is the story.

Lean on the seven phases, the four domains, and the value gap. Structure is the proof that this is managed, not improvised.

Seven phases. Eighteen to twenty-four months. Every step has a deliverable.
Principle 04

Write from the owner's chair.

Name what the owner controls and recognizes. Talk about their number, their gap, their terms, not our process language.
